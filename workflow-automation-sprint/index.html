How we work together

Twice a week we meet for two hours — to scope, review, refine, decide. Between sessions, I build. You're welcome to build alongside me. Early sessions lean toward direction-setting. Later sessions lean toward integration and handover. The four-week container is fixed; what happens inside it is shaped by your workflow.

I do the construction. You do the strategic and judgment work — defining scope, evaluating outputs, making the decisions only you can make. You don't have to write code.

Throughout, I show what I'm building and how. Every prompt, every pattern, every decision is documented as we go.

The workflow runs on Claude, Claude Code, and lightweight automation — whatever fits your stack — so it keeps running after the engagement ends.

Some installation is usually involved. That definitely includes Claude Code on your local machine(s). Depending on the workflow, that might also mean an automation tool like n8n or Zapier, scheduled scripts running where it fits your stack, or API connections to your existing marketing tools. We scope what's needed during the discovery call.

What you have at the end of week four

  1. A workflow that works. A workflow running in your stack, producing real outputs your team uses.

  2. The build, documented. The pipeline, the prompts, and the reasoning behind every decision — written in plain language for your team to read and extend.

  3. A handover. Final sessions walk your team through the workflow — how it runs in your stack, how to extend it, what to do when something breaks.

  4. The artifacts and the knowledge to attempt the next one. If your team wants to extend the workflow or build the next one, they have what they need to try.
