B2C media automation pipeline

Full lifecycle from context setup through campaign learning. AI handles the heavy lifting at every step. Humans decide at every gate. The knowledge base gets smarter each cycle.

Scope: paid digital (social, search, display, video, connected TV). Adjacent channels — OOH, influencer, publisher direct — follow the same pattern with channel-specific launch mechanics.

Automation triggers

Every step in the pipeline starts with something. These are the triggers that put the system in motion.

  • Scheduled. Nightly performance pull. Weekly strategic recap. Monthly attribution deep dive. Campaign end date triggers the Phase 06 knowledge base update. Quarterly competitive baseline refresh.
  • Threshold-based. Spend pacing 25%+ off plan pauses the campaign and notifies. CPA drift above target queues a creative refresh. Frequency above threshold proposes audience expansion. Statistically significant A/B test winners auto-report.
  • Event-based. Platform ad rejection routes the rework to creative. Client replies to an approval email and the gate advances (or revisions are queued). A lookalike audience matures and the system proposes including it in the next flight.
  • Signal-based. Competitor launches a new campaign, competitive briefing generated. Category-relevant news trends, the system proposes a creative angle. Seasonal pattern matches historical lift, a budget-push proposal is queued for the media director.
  • Manual and on-demand. Account lead posts in Slack: "summarize yesterday's Meta performance" — AI generates on demand. Media director asks for a fresh competitive scan, runs ad hoc. The system handles one-off requests without falling out of its scheduled rhythm.

Platform access and integrations

What the system can actually touch — and what still requires a human in the platform UI.

  • Meta (Facebook, Instagram). Marketing API for campaign creation, creative upload, reporting, and Conversions API (CAPI) for server-side tracking. Requires Business Manager admin access and app-level permissions. Some Advantage+ controls still require the UI.
  • Google (Ads, YouTube, DV360). Google Ads API for search, shopping, display, video. Display & Video 360 API for programmatic. Requires MCC (manager) account linkage. Search Ads 360 for cross-engine workflows.
  • TikTok. TikTok Marketing API for campaign setup, creative upload, and reporting. Requires Business Center partnership and app approval.
  • Programmatic and connected TV. The Trade Desk, Amazon DSP, and DV360 APIs for programmatic display, video, and CTV. Direct CTV buys (Netflix Ads, Hulu, Roku) vary — some API, some insertion-order.
  • Publisher direct and OOH. No API. AI handles asset prep, trafficking docs, and reporting consolidation; the buy itself goes through traditional rep channels.
  • Analytics and data. GA4 Data API, Segment, BigQuery or Snowflake for the warehouse. Conversions APIs (Meta CAPI, Google Enhanced Conversions) for server-side tracking where first-party data matters.
  • Credentials and audit. OAuth tokens rotated on schedule. Access scoped per agency seat, not shared logins. Every automated API call logged with the workflow and the human who triggered it. Full audit trail for every change the system makes.

When something breaks

Automation fails. Platforms reject ads. APIs go down. Data doesn't reconcile. These are the exception paths.

  • Platform API failures. Retries with exponential backoff. After three failures, escalates to the media buyer with the full error and the last-known state. Nothing silently fails.
  • Ad rejections. Rejection reasons route back to the creative phase. AI proposes a revised version; creative director approves before resubmitting. No silent fixes to creative.
  • Spend anomalies. Daily spend pacing 25%+ outside plan triggers an automatic pause on the affected campaign plus a notification. Human reviews before unpausing.
  • Data reconciliation. When platform-reported conversions and GA4 / CRM data diverge by more than 15%, the discrepancy appears in the next report rather than being silently resolved.
  • Client revisions. Feedback captured in a structured form, fed to the AI for one re-gen, then creative director reviews before the next client pass. Unstructured feedback is rewritten into the form first.

Additional automation opportunities

Adjacent workflows that read from the same knowledge base and extend the pipeline without duplicating context.

Competitor monitoring

Continuous feed of competitor ad changes, new creative, spend shifts. AI alerts when a competitor enters or exits a channel or launches a new campaign.

Landing page generation

AI generates campaign-specific landing pages from the creative brief and brand knowledge base. Copy, layout, and conversion elements built from the campaign's messaging and audience data.

Email sequence automation

AI writes post-click nurture sequences triggered by campaign conversions. Each email reads from the knowledge base — brand voice, audience rules, product messaging all consistent.

Organic social content

AI generates organic social posts that complement paid campaigns. Coordinated messaging, shared creative direction, published on schedule. Extends paid reach without additional ad spend.

Influencer brief generation

AI drafts influencer briefs from the campaign strategy — messaging guardrails, required disclosures, brand guidelines, content deliverables. Consistent across all partners.

Seasonal and event triggers

AI monitors calendar events, cultural moments, and trending topics. Proposes timely creative variations and budget shifts when an opportunity aligns with the brand's positioning.

Compounding

Each cycle through this pipeline feeds performance data, audience learnings, creative results, and competitive intelligence back into the knowledge base. A campaign on its third cycle starts from everything the first two learned. Next cycle starts sharper — not because the team got faster, but because the system got smarter.
