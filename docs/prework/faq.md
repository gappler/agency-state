# Cohort 1 FAQ

Quick answers to common questions about prework, install, and logistics. If yours isn't here, DM Greg in Slack or reply to any of the cohort emails.

---

## Subscription and install

### Do I need to pay for Claude Pro on top of the course fee?

Yes. Claude Pro is $20/month and is required to run Claude Code. You can cancel after the cohort if you don't want to keep it. Think of it as a one-month software expense tied to the program. (Most graduates keep it — the tool becomes part of how you work.)

### Can I use my free Claude account instead?

No. Free Claude accounts can't authenticate Claude Code. You need either a Claude Pro subscription or an Anthropic API key (pay-per-token billing, more technical). Claude Pro is the simpler path for most participants.

### Can I cancel Claude Pro after the cohort?

Yes. Cancel anytime before the next billing cycle. The cohort runs four sessions over two weeks plus one between-session build window, so one month of Pro is the minimum commitment.

### What if my work machine blocks npm installs?

Work with your IT department. Raise the request this week, not the week of Session 1 — some IT turnarounds are slow. If IT won't unblock, use a personal machine. The cohort assumes you can install dev tools on your primary machine; we can't work around a locked-down laptop live in class.

### What if I can't get the install working before Session 1?

Your IT team is the first stop for machine-level issues (permissions, npm blocked, locked-down laptop). DM Greg in Slack for Claude-Code-specific questions — authentication, CLI errors, "I installed but can't get it to run" — and we'll find time for a quick call. Showing up to Session 1 with a broken install puts you behind and slows the group, so don't wait.

## Data and security

### Is Claude Code safe to use with my company data?

Claude Code sends files in your project folder to Anthropic's servers for processing. By installing and using it, you agree to [Anthropic's policies](https://www.anthropic.com/legal). Read them.

**Before using any work data**, check your company's AI tool policy. If your organization has rules about AI tools, approved vendors, or data handling, those rules apply to you. Ask your security or compliance team if in doubt.

**Don't put the following in your project folder:** customer PII, production credentials, regulated data (HIPAA, PCI, etc.), trade secrets. If you're modeling your real company for the cohort, sanitize anything truly sensitive — the prework data is synthetic either way.

### Is Agency State responsible if something goes wrong with my install or data?

No. Agency State teaches the method. We don't manage your install, your data, or your company's IT environment. For installation and machine-level issues, contact your IT team. For questions about Claude Code itself, DM Greg in Slack. For anything related to Anthropic's terms or policies, refer to [anthropic.com/legal](https://www.anthropic.com/legal).

## Picking your company

### Can I use my real company, or should I invent one?

Either works. Real companies give you a portfolio-worthy build at the end that you can use at work. Simulated companies give you full creative freedom and avoid data sensitivity. Step 1 of prework walks you through the decision.

### What if my business is services, B2B, or nonprofit — not DTC?

The method works for any business shape. The Yowie reference is DTC because it's visually clear, but the prompt templates don't assume a product business. A B2B SaaS company's brand definition will emphasize ICP and MRR; an agency's will emphasize SOWs and utilization; a nonprofit's will emphasize programs and funding. All valid.

### How much detail does my brand definition need?

800–1500 words, six sections, concrete specifics (not placeholders). Compare against the Yowie reference — if yours has similar specificity and structure, you're ready.

### How do I know my synthetic data is "good enough"?

It should have: 12 monthly rows, 5–10 columns, realistic numbers for your business size, seasonality, and at least one month with a meaningful anomaly. The anomaly is the important part — it gives Session 2's planning work something real to analyze.

## Logistics

### What if I can't attend a session?

Sessions are recorded and posted same-day. But cohort-based learning depends heavily on live participation — the between-session build, peer feedback on Demo Day, shared momentum. If you know in advance you'll miss a session, reply and we'll talk through options. Unexpected absences happen; if that's you, watch the recording and come back next time.

### Are sessions recorded?

Yes. Recordings are posted the same day of the session and stay available throughout the program.

### What if I fall behind during the between-session build?

Use Wednesday's office hours before Session 3 as your reset point. Slack is also available 24/7 for stuck points. Don't disappear — we want you back. The between-session build is the centerpiece of Demo Day, so falling silent means losing your moment to show what you built.

## Learning

### Do I need any programming background?

No. The cohort assumes you can follow the install guide's step-by-step instructions (you'll use a terminal), but no coding knowledge is required. If you can follow careful technical documentation, you're ready.

### What if Claude Code produces bad output?

Expected and part of the process. Bad output is a signal that your prompt needs more context, more specificity, or a different angle. In Session 1 we practice the iteration loop that turns bad output into good. The ability to evaluate and iterate is the real skill — not expecting Claude to be right the first time.

### Do I need to know anything about AI before starting?

No, but fluency with Claude chat helps. If you've never had a real working conversation with Claude (or another frontier model), the prework will feel harder than it needs to. If that describes you, spend 30 minutes playing in Claude.ai's free tier first — just ask it to help you with something real from your job.
