# Company Selection

Before Session 1, you need to pick a company you'll work with for the full two weeks. You can use your real company or invent one. This step helps you decide.

**Time:** 10–15 minutes in Claude chat.
**Output:** A clear decision on which company you'll use, and — if you're inventing one — which archetype you'll build.

---

## How to use this prompt

1. Open [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) in a new tab. Start a fresh conversation.
2. Copy the prompt below.
3. Fill in the bracketed sections with your own context.
4. Paste into Claude and have the conversation.
5. When Claude recommends a direction, save its reply. You'll use it in Step 2 (Brand Definition).

---

## The prompt

```
I'm preparing for a two-week AI cohort called Agency State. I'll be building
marketing and strategic outputs using Claude Code. Before the cohort starts,
I need to pick a company I'll use throughout the program. Help me decide.

About me:
- Role: [your role — e.g., marketing director, agency strategist, founder,
  ops lead]
- Industry or context: [where you work — e.g., B2B SaaS, DTC outdoor gear,
  professional services agency, nonprofit]
- The work I produce most: [the outputs you create regularly — e.g.,
  dashboards, campaign plans, client reports, content calendars, strategy
  briefs, performance analyses]

My two options:

1. Use my real company. I'd bring real business context (product, audience,
   goals) and build outputs I could actually use at work.
   Tradeoff: some data may be sensitive; I may need to adapt or synthesize
   parts of it.

2. Use a simulated company. I'd invent a business with a similar shape to
   mine. Full creative freedom, no sensitivity concerns.
   Tradeoff: I start with less context and have to build the business up
   from scratch.

Please:
- Ask me 2–3 clarifying questions about my situation.
- Recommend real or simulated based on my answers.
- If you recommend simulated, suggest 3 company archetypes I could pick
  from. Each should let me practice outputs similar to what I produce at
  my real job, but from different angles (industry, size, business model).
- End with one clear recommendation and a short reason.

My goal is to arrive at Session 1 with a company in mind that will give me
meaningful practice AND a portfolio-worthy build at the end.
```

---

## What to save

After the conversation, save the following somewhere you can find it (a note app, a doc, or a file on your desktop):

- **Company name** (real or invented)
- **One-sentence description** of the company
- **Why you picked it** (Claude's reasoning or your own)

You'll use this in Step 2 to generate your full brand definition.

---

## If you get stuck

- **Claude keeps asking for more info instead of recommending:** Tell it "please make a recommendation now based on what you know."
- **None of the archetypes feel right:** Ask Claude for 3 more, or describe what's missing and let Claude riff.
- **You want to change companies mid-cohort:** Don't. The cohort assumes you stick with one. If you realize it's wrong, tell the facilitator before Session 2 and we'll regroup.
